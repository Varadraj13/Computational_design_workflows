Wong Kar-wai

Master of Mood, Memory, and Melancholy

Hong Kong's most celebrated auteur director, known for his distinctive visual style, atmospheric storytelling, and exploration of love, time, and urban alienation.

About the Director

Born: July 17, 1958, Shanghai, China

Nationality: Hong Kong

Style: Known for his moody, atmospheric films with distinctive cinematography, non-linear narratives, and themes of love, memory, and urban alienation.

Signature Elements: Slow motion sequences, neon-lit cityscapes, rain-soaked streets, voice-over narration, and collaborations with cinematographer Christopher Doyle.

Complete Filmography

As Tears Go By (旺角卡門)

1988

Crime Drama

A small-time gangster must choose between his criminal life and the woman he loves. Wong's debut feature introduces his signature visual style and themes of urban alienation.

Cast: Andy Lau, Maggie Cheung, Jacky Cheung

Runtime: 102 minutes

Days of Being Wild (阿飛正傳)

1990

Drama

A playboy drifter's relationships with two women in 1960s Hong Kong. The first film in Wong's informal trilogy about time and memory.

Cast: Leslie Cheung, Maggie Cheung, Andy Lau, Carina Lau

Runtime: 94 minutes

Chungking Express (重慶森林)

1994

Romance Drama

Two separate stories of love and loneliness in Hong Kong, featuring a cop obsessed with expiration dates and a mysterious woman in a blonde wig.

Cast: Brigitte Lin, Takeshi Kaneshiro, Tony Leung, Faye Wong

Runtime: 102 minutes

Ashes of Time (東邪西毒)

1994

Wuxia Drama

A reimagining of Jin Yong's wuxia novel, focusing on the emotional lives of swordsmen rather than action sequences. Features stunning desert cinematography.

Cast: Leslie Cheung, Tony Leung, Brigitte Lin, Maggie Cheung

Runtime: 100 minutes

Fallen Angels (墮落天使)

1995

Crime Drama

Two parallel stories: a hitman and his female partner, and a mute ex-convict. Shot in the neon-lit streets of Hong Kong with distinctive wide-angle cinematography.

Cast: Leon Lai, Michelle Reis, Takeshi Kaneshiro, Charlie Yeung

Runtime: 99 minutes

Happy Together (春光乍洩)

1997

Romance Drama

Two Hong Kong men's turbulent relationship while living in Buenos Aires. Won Best Director at Cannes Film Festival.

Cast: Tony Leung, Leslie Cheung, Chang Chen

Runtime: 96 minutes

In the Mood for Love (花樣年華)

2000

Romance Drama

Two neighbors in 1960s Hong Kong suspect their spouses are having an affair and begin their own relationship. Considered one of the greatest films ever made.

Cast: Tony Leung, Maggie Cheung

Runtime: 98 minutes

2046 (2046)

2004

Sci-Fi Romance

A writer's relationships with various women, intercut with scenes from his science fiction novel about a train to the year 2046.

Cast: Tony Leung, Zhang Ziyi, Gong Li, Faye Wong

Runtime: 129 minutes

My Blueberry Nights (藍莓之夜)

2007

Romance Drama

A young woman's journey across America, meeting various characters and discovering herself. Wong's first English-language film.

Cast: Norah Jones, Jude Law, Natalie Portman, Rachel Weisz

Runtime: 95 minutes

The Grandmaster (一代宗師)

2013

Martial Arts Drama

The life story of Ip Man, the legendary martial arts master who trained Bruce Lee. Features stunning action sequences and period detail.

Cast: Tony Leung, Zhang Ziyi, Chang Chen

Runtime: 130 minutes

Blossoms Shanghai (繁花)

2023

Drama

A television series adaptation of Jin Yucheng's novel about Shanghai in the 1990s, following the rise and fall of a businessman during China's economic boom.

Cast: Hu Ge, Ma Yili, Tang Yan

Runtime: 30 episodes

Awards and Recognition

  • Cannes Film Festival: Best Director for "Happy Together" (1997)
  • Hong Kong Film Awards: Best Director for "In the Mood for Love" (2001)
  • Asian Film Awards: Lifetime Achievement Award (2013)
  • Venice Film Festival: Golden Lion nomination for "In the Mood for Love" (2000)
  • Academy Awards: Hong Kong's official submission for "In the Mood for Love" (2000)
